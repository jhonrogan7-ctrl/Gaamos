Guest menu v2 — layout exploration

Three variants, one component kit. Same Sherpa House (testco) content, same approved themes. V-A keeps today's rail + accordion, rebuilt on the polished kit. V-B moves categories to top tabs with one continuous scroll-spy list. V-C shrinks the rail to icons and moves subcategories to thumb-reach chips. Pick per-screen or whole-variant; mixing allowed (e.g. V-B navigation + V-A cards). Every variant bakes in the audit fixes (F1–F14) listed at the bottom of this page.

Kit fixes baked into every variant

  • F1 — first category open on load (all mocks assume categories[0], no hardcoded id).
  • F2 — venue monogram generated from the name; no hardcoded logo anywhere.
  • F3 — detail CTA sits in its own layer above the body; content can never paint over it.
  • F4 — all add/stepper controls ≥44px (kit deviation: qty buttons 44px, not the spec'd 36px).
  • F5/F6 — photo-less cards get a deliberate category-icon monogram tile, same geometry; wrong-subject photos fall back to it rather than shipping mismatched stock.
  • F7 — promo code and sort-by are omitted until they exist; no dead controls.
  • F9 — dish name may wrap 2 lines; price has its own reserved column and never collides.
  • F11/F12 — persistent order-placed screen with prominent number, status pill and receipt; positive check badge, nothing trash-like.
  • F13 — footer/copyright is the venue's own; no donor or agency branding.
  • Deviation: .k-price uses the theme's --price variable (not accent-deep) to honour the approved CSS-var theme contract.
  • Deviation: rail variants (V-A/V-C) use the compact card — 72px photo, price stacked on its own row (F9) — because the rail leaves a ~232px column; V-B's full-width list shows the full 88px kit card. Photo-less geometry stays identical in both.

What each variant answers

  • V-A Baseline+ — keeps the audit's praised rail + accordion wayfinding untouched; pure component polish. Lowest-risk pick.
  • V-B Top tabs — full-width cards (~88px wider), one continuous browse, sticky section headers give in-category wayfinding for mega-categories (F10); shows the "Others" group fix for un-subcategorised dishes (F14 — Mixed Grill Platter).
  • V-C Icon rail + chips — keeps the always-visible category map but returns ~32px of card width; subcategory switching moves into thumb reach (chips), helping one-handed use (F4's spirit).
